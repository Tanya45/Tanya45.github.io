WATER SCARCITY

  • by:Tanya Dubey
  • date:June 8,2019
  • Comments

water.. you might have heard that we are running out of fresh water .This might sound strange to you because,if you live in a place where water flows freely out of shower or taps anytime, it sure doesn’t seems like a big deal. it just there right?.

The only obvious thing about fresh water is that how much we need it.Because It is essential to life we need to think about it carefully.Right now,at this very moment,many people,womens and girls in particular,walk hours and miles per day to get fresh water,and even then it may not be clean. Every 15 seconds a child dies due to water borne-diseases. This is tragic.The most compelling reasons to think about fresh water,therefore have to do with what we might call,the global common good.This is not something we normally think about but it means thinking about how much fresh water matters for the flourishing of human and other form of life on earth now and in the future. Many people assume that fresh water shortages are due to individual wastefullness:running the water while you brush your teeth for example or taking really long showers.Most of us assume such that water sortages can be fixed by improving our personal habits:taking shorter showers of turning of the taps while we brush our teeth.

“Don't flush our planet's most valuable resource.

But global fresh water scarcity neither starts of ends with our showers.Globally domestic use of fresh water only accounts for 8% of consumption.compare that to 70% that goes to agriculture and 22 % that goes to industrial uses. Now,hold up-you’re not off the hook! individual habits are still part of the puzzle.You should still cultivate water virtue in your daily life,turn off the taps when you brush your teeth.but still it’s true taking shorter showers won’t solve global problems,which is too bad.It would be much more straightforward and easier if virtuous, individual actions could do the trick.You’d just stand there for 30 seconds less,and you’d be done with that irksome,planet -saving task of the day.Well that’s not so much the case.Agricultural and industrial patterns of water use need serious attention.how do our society value water?Distribute it.Subsidize its use in agriculture.Incentivize its consumption or pollution? These are all Questions that stem from how we think about fresh water’s value.IsIt an economic commodity?A human right?A Public good? Nobel prize winners,Global water justice activists,Transnational Institutions like United Nations,are at work on the issue.But it’s ,tricky too,because the business of water became very profitable in the 20th century.And profit is not the same thing as the common Good.We need to figure out how to use water as the public good,something that is vital for human and non-human ,now and in the future,now that’s a virtuous collective task that goes far beyond your shower.
